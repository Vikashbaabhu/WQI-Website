About the tool

Our motive behind the visualization of Indian rivers

The Water Quality Index (WQI) is an essential tool for assessing the health of India's rivers, offering a thorough evaluation of water quality. The index is computed by considering key parameters such as dissolved oxygen, biochemical oxygen demand, pH, and nutrient levels, providing a numerical representation that reflects the overall condition of the water. Beyond identifying pollution sources, the WQI guides the development of targeted strategies for sustainable river management.

Moreover, advancements in water management strategies encompass the ability to predict trends in the Water Quality Index (WQI) and other relevant parameters. Utilizing innovative techniques, researchers can input specific years to anticipate potential shifts in water quality over time. This predictive modeling contributes to a comprehensive understanding of the long-term trajectory of WQI and associated factors, enabling proactive planning and timely interventions. By forecasting trends, authorities can implement preventive measures to address emerging challenges, ensuring a more sustainable and resilient approach to the conservation and management of India's rivers.

On another front, map visualization, employing latitude and longitude coordinates, enriches our comprehension of the intricate dynamics of these rivers. It provides a graphical representation of river courses, confluences, and environmental features, serving as a visual tool for researchers, policymakers, and communities. This dynamic approach enables prompt responses to environmental changes and supports well-informed decision-making. The integration of WQI calculations with map visualization offers stakeholders a holistic perspective on the health of India's rivers. This combined approach assists in pinpointing pollution-prone areas, understanding spatial patterns of water quality degradation, and formulating region-specific conservation strategies. Ultimately, it empowers authorities to proactively implement measures for mitigating pollution, ensuring the sustainable management of water resources and safeguarding the ecological integrity of these vital river systems.
